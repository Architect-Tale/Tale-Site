Artifacts

Magical Artifacts consist of four parts:

  • A metal surface
  • A focus
  • A script
  • And a caster

    •  
    The Metal Surface is typically what you makes up the bulk of the artifact. While metal can be expensive to acquire and difficult to work with in comparison to glass, its arcane nature makes it a perfect conduit for both mana and script. Nearly any metal will suffice for an artifact, and in any amount (so long as the full script for the spell can be inscribed along its length.

    You will often see spells bend and twist their way across metal bands to try to maximize surface space for script and minimize the amount of precious metal consumed). The type of metal may influence the resulting spell's duration or how quickly it burns through the caster's mana reserves.
